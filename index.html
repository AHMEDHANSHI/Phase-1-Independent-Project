Who are the Specialists?

Specialists are persons who focus mainly on one specific area of practice, subject, or activity. A specialist may also be described as a very skilled individual in a restricted field. In the field of medicine, a medical specialist is one who is involved in a branch of medical practice dedicated to a well-defined or specific set of diseases, skills, patients, or philosophy.

surgery, nephrology, cardiology, or orthopediology are examples of specialized areas of medical practice. We tend to refer to all of them as plainly doctors; however, many of these “doctors” possess additional qualifications in other areas of medicine. To date, there are hundreds of specialized medical fields.

Throughout our lifetime, many of us will, at one point, require the help of a medical specialist. At that time, it is essential that you make an informed decision. Before settling on a choice, discuss the possible candidates with your primary healthcare provider. Also, the following are key factors that should influence your decision:

  • Will the health professional work in partnership with you?
  • Is the health professional board-certified?
  • Is the health professional well-trained and experienced?
  • Does your health plan provide coverage for this service?
  • Will the health professional be available when needed?
